# Lazynext — Business Model Canvas

> **Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** April 15, 2026 | **Product:** Lazynext v0.4.0
> **Tagline:** "Stop Switching Apps. Start Shipping Work."

---

## Overview

Lazynext is a **graph-native workflow platform** that unifies tasks, docs, decisions, threads, and automations on an infinite canvas. Positioned as "The Anti-Software Workflow Platform," it replaces 6+ standalone tools with a single, AI-powered workspace. Its hero feature — **Decision DNA** — is a first-of-its-kind system that logs, scores, and tracks team decisions over time.

## 1. Customer Segments

### Primary Segments

| Segment | Description | Size Signal |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Remote-first SaaS teams (5–50 people)** | Engineering, product, and design teams drowning in tool-switching overhead. Currently juggling Notion + Linear + Slack + Zapier + Sheets. | Core ICP |
| **Digital agencies** | Client-facing teams managing multiple projects with cross-functional workflows, needing consolidated project visibility. | Secondary ICP |
| **Indie hackers & solo operators** | Individual builders who need lightweight project management without enterprise bloat. Entry point via Free tier. | Acquisition funnel |
| **Startups (seed to Series A)** | Early-stage companies that want to build good decision-making habits from day one. Price-sensitive, value consolidation. | Growth segment |

### Secondary Segments

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Enterprise teams (50–500 people)** | Organizations needing SSO, audit logs, API access, and custom SLA. Served via Business/Enterprise tiers. |
| **Global/emerging-market teams** | Teams in India and other cost-sensitive markets. Lazynext's global pricing in 56 currencies and 40 languages is a key differentiator here. |

### Customer Characteristics
- Teams using **4–6 different productivity tools** simultaneously
- Frustrated by **decisions lost in Slack threads** and **context scattered across apps**
- Value **async-first, remote-friendly** collaboration
- Willing to consolidate tools to **reduce per-seat SaaS spend**

### Unique Differentiators

| Differentiator | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|---------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Decision DNA** | AI-scored decision tracking (0–100 quality score across 4 dimensions), outcome logging (good/bad/neutral), searchable decision graph | "Nothing else does this." Teams can learn from past decisions and build institutional memory. |
| **Graph-native canvas** | Infinite canvas with 7 node types connected via edges, not a flat list or board | Workflows are visual and relational; context flows between connected nodes. |

| **7 unified primitives** | Task, Doc, Decision, Thread, Pulse, Automation, Table — each replacing a standalone tool | One mental model replaces six. |
| **Tool consolidation economics** | $19/seat/mo vs. $171/seat/mo for 6 separate tools | 89% cost reduction is an immediate, quantifiable ROI. |
| **Global-first architecture** | 40 languages, 56 currencies, locale-aware pricing from day one | Competitors charge USD-only; Lazynext meets teams where they are. |

## 9. Cost Structure

### Fixed Costs

| Cost Category | Description | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Team compensation** | 3-person team (CEO, Design, Engineering) | Largest fixed cost; lean team |
| **Infrastructure (base)** | Supabase (database + auth), Vercel hosting, domain | Scales with usage but has a baseline |
| **SaaS tools** | Development tools, monitoring, communications | Standard startup overhead |

### Variable Costs (Scale with Usage)

| Cost Category | Driver | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| **AI inference (Groq + Together AI)** | Per AI query (up to 500/day on Pro) | Largest variable cost; scales directly with AI usage |
| **Supabase database** | Storage, bandwidth, and connection count | Grows with users and data volume |
| **Lemon Squeezy fees** | % of revenue (Merchant of Record cut) | Fixed percentage of all transactions |
| **Resend email** | Per email sent (invites, digests, notifications) | Moderate; weekly digest is the largest volume |
| **Inngest** | Per function invocation (6 background functions) | Moderate; scales with decision logging and exports |
| **Vercel hosting** | Bandwidth, serverless function invocations | Scales with traffic |

### Cost Optimization Strategies

| Strategy | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **AI query limits by tier** | Free: 20/day, Starter: 100/day, Pro: 500/day — prevents runaway AI costs |
| **Node limits by tier** | Free: 100, Starter: 1K, Pro: 10K — controls database growth on free/low tiers |
| **Groq + Together AI dual-provider** | Primary/fallback pattern optimizes for cost and reliability |
| **Inngest serverless** | Pay-per-invocation background jobs vs. always-on workers |
| **Lemon Squeezy as MoR** | Offloads tax compliance, fraud prevention, and global payment infrastructure |
| **Lean team (3 people)** | AI-assisted development (Claude agent) multiplies engineering capacity |

### Unit Economics Model

| Metric | Estimate | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **CAC** | Low (product-led growth) | Free tier + viral sharing + SEO |
| **LTV** | High (team-based SaaS) | Per-seat pricing × team size × retention |
| **Gross margin** | ~70–80% (target) | Primary costs are AI inference and infrastructure |
| **Payback period** | <3 months (target) | Low-touch acquisition; fast conversion via trial |

## Strategic Insights

### Strengths
1. **Decision DNA is a true moat** — no competitor tracks decision quality with AI scoring. This is defensible and compounds over time as teams build decision history.
2. **Consolidation economics are compelling** — $19 vs. $171/seat/mo is a concrete, measurable ROI that sells itself.
3. **Global-first from day one** — 40 languages and 56 currencies is rare for a pre-Series-A product; unlocks underserved markets.
4. **Lean cost structure** — 3-person team with AI-assisted development keeps burn rate low.
5. **Product-led growth engine** — Free tier + 14-day trial + viral sharing + usage-based upgrade triggers create a self-sustaining funnel.

### Risks & Challenges
1. **Solo-developer velocity constraint** — Small team must prioritize ruthlessly; can't compete on feature breadth with well-funded competitors.
2. **AI inference costs** — Groq/Together AI costs scale directly with user growth; must monitor unit economics as AI usage increases.
3. **Consolidation paradox** — Replacing 6 tools means competing with 6 incumbents simultaneously (Notion, Linear, Slack, Zapier, Asana, Airtable).
4. **Enterprise readiness** — SSO, audit logs, and on-premise deployment are gated to higher tiers but may need significant investment to be production-grade.
5. **Integration depth** — Many listed integrations are UI-ready but not yet connected; delivery gap could erode trust.

### Growth Opportunities
1. **Decision DNA as a standalone API** — License decision tracking to other tools (potential platform play).
2. **Template marketplace monetization** — User-generated templates with revenue sharing.
3. **Vertical-specific workflows** — Pre-built templates for specific industries (agencies, SaaS, consulting).
4. **Mobile app** — Currently responsive web only; native mobile could unlock new use cases.
5. **Real-time collaboration** — Cursor overlays and live editing (planned Phase 2) would close a gap vs. Notion/Figma.
