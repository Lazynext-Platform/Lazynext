# Lazynext v1.0.0.0 — Release Notes

> **Shipped**: 2026-04-18
> **Codename**: *The Decision Intelligence Platform*
> **Thesis**: A team's ability to make good decisions is its most valuable compounding asset. Almost nobody measures it.

---

## TL;DR

Lazynext v1.0.0.0 is a decision intelligence platform. Every decision your team makes gets scored on four dimensions by a real LLM, tracked to its expected outcome, and reviewed when reality lands. New workspaces start minimal and unlock features as the team proves they're actually deciding, not accumulating seats.

The platform surface (tasks, docs, threads, canvas, automations, tables) is still here. It's now gated by a **Workspace Maturity Score** so teams don't get an empty 20-feature tool on day one.

---

## Why this exists

Every team we've worked with has the same problem, and nobody sells the solution.

- Linear tells you *what* to do.
- Notion tells you *how* you did it.
- Slack tells you *who* said what.
- Nobody tells you *why* you chose the thing you chose, or whether it worked.

Decisions are the highest-leverage work a team does, and they're the least instrumented. A product decision today costs you six months of engineering in Q3. A hiring decision this week costs you a year. When the outcome lands, nobody goes back to the decision and asks "did we reason about this well, or did we get lucky?"

Teams don't learn from decisions because decisions aren't a first-class object anywhere.

Lazynext makes decisions a first-class object. That's the whole game.

## What's new in 1.0.0.0

### Decision DNA, 4-dimension AI scoring

Every decision gets scored by an LLM on four equally weighted dimensions (0 to 100 each):

- **Clarity** — is the question sharp, or a vague vibe?
- **Data quality** — is the rationale grounded in evidence or in guesses?
- **Risk awareness** — does it name the downside, the reversibility, the stakes?
- **Alternatives considered** — what did you seriously weigh, and what did you reject?

Primary model is Groq's `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` (`groq:llama-3.3-70b-v2` in our versioning). Together AI is the fallback. If both are unavailable or the response is unparseable, a deterministic heuristic takes over so scoring never blocks a decision from being logged.

See [lib/ai/decision-scorer.ts](../../lib/ai/decision-scorer.ts), including the `extractJson()` function that strips markdown fences and surrounding prose before parsing, because Llama will wrap JSON in ` ```json ` fences no matter how firmly you tell it not to.

The 4-dim breakdown renders inline on every decision via the quality badge. Every score is stamped with `score_model_version` so when you look back in a year you know which model judged it.

### Public decision pages, `/d/[slug]`

Opt-in shareable decision pages at `/d/[slug]`. Only decisions with `is_public = true` render. Full OG metadata for social sharing, dimension breakdown bars, 5-minute ISR revalidation so shared links stay fresh without hammering the DB.

Share a decision on Twitter. Post it in the RFC channel. Put it in the quarterly review. The scoring makes the reasoning legible even to people who weren't in the room.

### Workspace Maturity Score (WMS), progressive feature exposure

New workspaces don't need 7 node types, 20 pages, and an automation builder. They need to log one decision. So we gate the UI on a score that grows with real usage:

| Layer | Score | Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| **L1 Entry** | 0+ | Decisions + outcomes only |
| **L2 Activation** | 15+ | Tasks + threads |
| **L3 Expansion** | 35+ | Docs + tables |
| **L4 Power** | 60+ | Canvas + automations + integrations |

WMS events and weights ([lib/wms.ts](../../lib/wms.ts)):

| Event | Weight |
|---|---|
| `decision_created` | +2 |
| `outcome_recorded` | +3 |
| `teammate_invited` | +5 |
| `decision_public_shared` | +2 |
| `integration_connected` | +4 |

Want the full kitchen on day one? Toggle `power_user_override`. The "Show all N sections" button in the sidebar flips it optimistically. The default bias is *earn the complexity*.

WMS has its own API: `GET /api/v1/workspace/[slug]/wms` reads score and layer, `PATCH` toggles the override. A `WmsHydrator` component loads state into the workspace store on boot without blocking the layout.

### The outcome loop

Decisions without outcomes are diary entries. Lazynext closes the loop:

- **`expected_by`** on every decision, when you expect to know if this worked
- **Daily Inngest scan** (`handleOutcomeReminderScan`), finds decisions past their `expected_by` date and emails the author
- **`outcome_reminder_sent_at`** stamp, prevents reminder storms when a decision sits unreviewed for weeks
- **Weekly digest rewrite** (`handleWeeklyDigest`), now summarizes decisions logged, outcomes recorded, and WMS progression per workspace, not just generic activity
- **Decision logged hook** (`handleDecisionLogged`), fires AI scoring on create, stamps `score_breakdown`, `score_rationale`, `score_model_version`, and increments workspace WMS in the same transaction

The reminder isn't a marketing nudge. It's "hey, you said you'd know in 30 days. It's been 30 days. What happened?"

### Global `Cmd+Shift+D`

Log a decision from anywhere in the app. Not buried three clicks deep in a submenu. The shortcut lives in `GlobalLogDecisionShortcut` and is always mounted.

### Schema migration

`lib/db/migrations/00002_decision_intelligence_spine.sql`:

- **`decisions`**: `expected_by`, `score_breakdown` (jsonb), `is_public`, `public_slug`, `score_model_version`, `score_rationale`, `outcome_reminder_sent_at`
- **`workspaces`**: `wms_score`, `wms_updated_at`, `power_user_override`
- **`nodes`**: `decision_id`, `operational_reason` + `nodes_decision_spine_check` constraint. Every operational node now has to say either "this is connected to a decision" or "this is operational, here's why"

### 20 new tests

Unit coverage on WMS thresholds, feature unlocks, power user override, and decision scorer JSON parsing robustness (plain JSON, ` ```json ` fences, bare ` ``` ` fences, prose-wrapped responses, out-of-range clamping, AI-failure fallback, weighted aggregate math), plus E2E regression on `/d/[slug]`.

## Fixes worth calling out

- **ISSUE-001**: `/careers`, `/contact`, `/docs`, and `/d/[slug]` all linked to `/signup` (404). 4 link sites updated to the real `/sign-up` route.
- **ISSUE-002**: The decision scorer silently fell back to heuristic every time Llama wrapped JSON in ` ```json ... ``` ` fences. `extractJson()` now strips fences and isolates the outermost `{…}` from prose. The "AI scores on 4 dimensions" headline now actually runs on AI.
- **ISSUE-003**: First-run UX hung for roughly 7.6 seconds on every DB-touching page when `.env.local` held stock placeholder values. Placeholder env detection (`your-project`, `your-service-role`, `example.supabase.co`, literal `placeholder`, `your-api-key`) now returns safe fallbacks in under 250ms.

## Migration notes

- Run `supabase db push` (or equivalent) to apply `00002_decision_intelligence_spine.sql`.
- Existing decisions without `score_breakdown` still render. The badge gracefully degrades to the legacy single-score display.
- New workspaces start at `wms_score = 0` (L1). Existing workspaces keep their current surface. WMS is additive.
- Set `GROQ_API_KEY` in `.env.local` to enable AI scoring. Without it, scoring falls back to the heuristic path automatically. No crashes, no blocked decisions.

## What's in the box

### Decision DNA

Every decision is a typed node with structured fields: context, options, chosen path, reasoning, reversibility, stakes, stakeholders. An AI quality score (0-100) reads the structure and flags weak reasoning, missing alternatives, or under-specified stakes. When the outcome lands, you tag it, and the decision gets a retrospective score. Over time you see decision quality trends by person, team, and category.

This is the platform's reason to exist. Everything else orbits it.

### The canvas

An infinite ReactFlow graph. Seven node types: Task, Doc, Decision, Thread, Pulse, Automation, Table. Every node can connect to every other node. You can see the decision that spawned the task that shipped the feature that hit the pulse dashboard, all on one screen. Below 640px the canvas gracefully falls back to a list view because a graph on a phone is malpractice.

### LazyMind

An AI panel powered by Groq with Together as fallback. Generates tasks from docs, drafts decisions from threads, proposes templates from empty canvases. Fast enough to feel local.

### Everything else

- **Threads** — discussions attached to any node, not a separate app
- **Pulse dashboard** — team health metrics with charts that mean something
- **Automation builder** — visual WHEN/THEN rules, no separate Zapier subscription
- **Templates** — 6 starter workspaces you can fork in one click
- **Imports** — Notion, Linear, Trello, with conflict resolution
- **Exports** — full workspace JSON/CSV archive, your data stays yours
- **40 locales** — native translations for 8 of them, RTL for Arabic
- **Global-first billing** — Lemon Squeezy handles tax and compliance in every country
