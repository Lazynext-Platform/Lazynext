## Blog post, long form (lives at `/blog/launching-lazynext`)

**Title:** Lazynext is live, and we think we shipped the thing PM tools forgot

**Subhead:** Every decision your team makes is now a scored, tracked, reviewed object. Here's why that matters more than another Kanban board.

---

Here's the uncomfortable thing about running a team.

You don't get paid for the tasks you complete. You get paid for the decisions you make. The hire. The architecture choice. The feature cut. The customer you said no to. Every one of those decisions costs or compounds for months, sometimes years.

And yet your decisions live in Slack threads that age out in three weeks, in meeting notes nobody reads, in the head of whoever was in the room. The outcome lands six months later and nobody goes back. Did we reason about this well? Did we get lucky? Would we do it again? Nobody knows. So the next decision gets made the same way, with the same blind spots, by the same people who never learned.

We built Lazynext because we were tired of this.

**The thesis.** A team's ability to make good decisions is its most valuable compounding asset. And almost nobody measures it. So we made decisions a first-class object, and we made an LLM grade them.

**Decision DNA.** Every decision in Lazynext gets scored on four equally weighted dimensions, 0 to 100 each:

- *Clarity* — is the question sharp, or a vague vibe?
- *Data quality* — is the rationale grounded in evidence or in guesses?
- *Risk awareness* — does it name the downside, the reversibility, the stakes?
- *Alternatives considered* — what did you seriously weigh, and what did you reject?

The primary scorer is Groq's Llama 3.3 70B. Together AI is the fallback. If both fail, a deterministic heuristic takes over, so the scorer never blocks you from logging a decision. Every score is stamped with the model version. Look back in a year and you'll know which model judged what.

**The outcome loop is the point.** A scored decision without a tracked outcome is a prettier diary. So every decision has an `expected_by` field. A daily Inngest job finds decisions past their date and emails the author. "Hey, you said you'd know in 30 days. It's been 30 days. What happened?" You tag the outcome. The decision gets a retrospective. Over time you see which categories your team wins at, which ones you fumble, and who has calibrated judgment versus who is guessing.

**Public decision pages.** Any decision can be shared at `/d/[slug]` with full OG metadata. Post it in the RFC channel. Put it in the quarterly review. Share it on Twitter. The scoring makes the reasoning legible even to people who weren't in the room. This is the thing every eng org has been faking with Google Docs for a decade.

**Workspace Maturity Score.** Most workflow tools give you 20 features on day one and hope you figure it out. We do the opposite. New workspaces get decisions and outcomes only. As you actually decide things, a score grows in the background and unlocks more: tasks and threads at 15 points, docs and tables at 35, the full canvas and automations at 60. Each action has a weight. Logging a decision is +2. Recording an outcome is +3. Inviting a teammate is +5. If you're a power user who wants everything immediately, flip one switch. The default bias is *earn the complexity*.

**What shipped.** 38 features. 72 polish commits after feature freeze. WCAG 2.1 AA. 40 locales. 57 currencies. Global billing via Lemon Squeezy. 20 new tests in this release on top of the existing suite, all green. Rate limiting on every API route (now 20). Error boundaries on every page. The full Decision DNA spine, from schema migration to the UI.

**What didn't.** Real-time collaboration cursors are plumbed but not battle-tested, so we're holding them for Q3. Native mobile isn't a product question, it's a distribution question. Self-hosted exists in the code but the support model doesn't.

**Try it.** `git clone`, `npm install`, `npm run dev`. Dev auth bypass is default, so you walk the entire UI with no database. Set `GROQ_API_KEY` to see the AI scorer. Without the key, the heuristic path still runs, still useful, just not AI.

If your team makes a lot of decisions and has no way to know if it's getting better at them, we want to talk to you. [hello@lazynext.com](mailto:hello@lazynext.com).

Go decide something good. Then come back and see if you did.

## Notes for whoever runs the launch

- **Lead with the thesis, not the feature list.** "Decisions are the highest-leverage work, and they're the least instrumented" is the hook. Don't bury it.
- **Decision DNA is the thing.** The 4 dimensions are what make it legible to non-users: clarity, data quality, risk awareness, alternatives considered. Say them out loud. Repeat them.
- **The outcome loop is what separates this from "AI scores your Slack message."** Without `expected_by` + the reminder cron, it's a party trick. With it, it's a feedback loop.
- **Progressive exposure is a feature, not a limitation.** Lead with "new workspaces don't drown in 20 features on day one" rather than "we hide things from you."
- **The dev auth bypass is your demo weapon.** Lead with it anywhere devs will see the post (HN, Reddit, X).
- **Call out what didn't ship.** Honesty about cursors and mobile is a feature, not a bug. Builders trust teams that say what they don't have.
- **The ask is specific.** "If your team makes a lot of decisions and has no way to know if it's getting better at them, we want to talk." Better than "sign up today."
